SECTION 1: THE HOUSTON PATIO PROBLEM
================================================================================

(Why 90% of Patio Advice Doesn't Apply to Katy Homeowners)

Here's something most contractors won't tell you:

Houston is one of the HARDEST places in America to build a
patio that lasts.

It's not because contractors here are bad. It's because of three
factors that make our area uniquely challenging:

FACTOR #1: THE CLAY SOIL MONSTER

Houston sits on some of the most expansive clay soil in the country.
When it rains, this soil swells. When it's dry, it contracts. This
constant movement puts THOUSANDS of pounds of pressure on
concrete slabs.

A patio built in Arizona or Colorado can get away with minimal
preparation. Try that here, and within 3-5 years you'll have cracks,
heaving, and a patio that looks 20 years old.

FACT: Houston clay soil can move 2-4 inches seasonally. That's
enough to crack even the thickest concrete if it's not properly
reinforced and prepared.

FACTOR #2: THE 50-INCH RAIN PROBLEM

Houston averages over 50 inches of rain per year. That's more than
Seattle. More than Miami. More than almost anywhere else in the
continental United States.

This means drainage isn't optional—it's life or death for your patio
AND your home's foundation. A patio that slopes toward your house
or creates standing water isn't just annoying—it's a $10,000+
foundation repair waiting to happen.

FACTOR #3: THE SUBDIVISION ACCESS CHALLENGE

Here's a dirty secret: Most Katy subdivisions were designed with
4-foot gates between houses. That's fine for lawnmowers. It's a
nightmare for concrete trucks.

Difficult access = more labor = higher costs. And if a contractor
doesn't account for this upfront, you'll get a surprise bill at the
end—or worse, they'll cut corners to stay on budget.

THE BOTTOM LINE:

Generic patio advice from the internet will fail you in Houston.
You need information specific to our soil, our rain, and our
subdivision layouts.

That's exactly what this guide provides.

SECTION 2: THE 3 FALSE BELIEFS KILLING YOUR PATIO BUDGET
================================================================================

Before we talk about costs, we need to address three misconceptions
that cause Katy homeowners to either overpay OR get a patio that
fails within years.

FALSE BELIEF #1: "Cheaper Per Square Foot = Better Deal"

This is the #1 mistake homeowners make.

Here's the truth: A contractor who quotes $25/sq ft instead of
$35/sq ft isn't giving you a better deal. They're either:

A) Skipping proper soil preparation (your patio will crack)
B) Using inadequate reinforcement (your patio will heave)
C) Planning to hit you with "surprise" charges later
D) All of the above

REAL EXAMPLE: A homeowner in Cinco Ranch got quotes from three
contractors. Two quoted $32-38/sq ft. One quoted $22/sq ft.
Excited about the savings, they went with the cheap option.

18 months later, they had 6 major cracks and a section that had
heaved 2 inches above the rest. The "fix" cost $4,200—bringing
their total cost HIGHER than the original $38/sq ft quote.

THE TRUTH: In Houston, quality patio installation has a floor.
Below that floor, you're buying a problem, not a patio.

---

FALSE BELIEF #2: "I Can Just Pour Over My Existing Patio"

This sounds logical. The old patio is already there—why not just
add a fresh layer on top?

Here's why it fails in Houston:

Our clay soil causes slabs to move independently. When you pour
new concrete over old, you create TWO slabs that move at different
rates. The bond between them breaks. The new layer cracks. Water
gets between the layers and accelerates the damage.

Within 2-3 years, your "new" patio looks worse than what you
started with.

THE TRUTH: Overlays CAN work in dry, stable climates. In Houston,
proper removal and replacement is almost always the smarter
long-term investment.

---

FALSE BELIEF #3: "All Concrete Is Basically the Same"

This is like saying "all cars are basically the same."

Technically, yes—all concrete is cement, aggregate, and water.
But the similarities end there.

In Houston, the differences that matter are:
- Concrete strength (PSI rating)
- Rebar spacing and gauge
- Base preparation depth
- Control joint placement
- Curing time and methods

A "basic" patio installed properly in Houston will outperform a
"premium" patio installed improperly by decades.

THE TRUTH: It's not about the concrete. It's about how it's
installed, and whether the contractor understands Houston's
unique challenges.
