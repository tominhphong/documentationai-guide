My Rules For Pricing For Max Profit 
Before I show you ten plays | use in my portfolios to tweak pricing to make more money, it’s 
worth explaining a few of my beliefs first, ‘They are the foundation upon which all the tactics are 


built. 


Rules of Pricing I Follow 

Lower prices convert more customers than higher prices. But, the drop off as you go 
up isn’t always one to one. Ex: If you double your price from $10 to $20, you often don’t cut your 
conversions in half. This discrepancy is fundamentally what all price increases take advantage of to 
make more profit. 

High close rate = prices too low. If you are closing over 50% of sales consistently, you 
usually need to raise your prices. (Unless you have another goal besides ‘make the most money’). 

Full capacity = prices too low, If you are at full capacity, but you are not profitable, raise 
your prices before expanding, 

Fixed Capacity. If you have a fixed capacity, and you are full, raise your prices. For example, 
if you are an artist and physically can’t paint more than 10 paintings per month, then you have fixed 
capacity. If they all sell out every month, you need to increase your prices. 

Raising Prices Is Easier Than Lowering Them. When you lower them you have to go 
back to people who are paying more and explain yourself. 

You Can Raise Prices More Times Than You Think. Some markets are price sensitive. 
Others less so. But 9 times out of 10, when you raise your prices, you make more profit than you 
lose in sales. 

Products Have Different Values At Different Times. If you worked in the sun all day 
without any water, a cold bottle of water has a lot of value. But after you drink that first bottle, a 
second bottle is significantly less valuable. A third even less so. When you offer a product to a 
customer will affect how much they're willing to pay for it. You want to offer it to them at the point 
of greatest need, not the point of greatest satisfaction. 

Products Have Different Values To Different Customers. If you improve a company 
doing $100,000,000 per year by 10%, it’s worth $10,000,000 to that company. If you do the same 
work for a $1,000,000 company, it’s worth $100,000 to them, Same work. Different customers. 


Different values, Perfect pricing accounts for this discrepancy to maximize profits and sell both.

Raising Prices Makes You Moncy In Two Ways. You make more gross profit per 


customer and you have less cost to deliver because you have fewer customers. So raising prices 


increases revenue and decreases costs at the same time. Ex: | /7p/ed the prices at my gym from $99 to 
$299 and I lost 30% of my customers. So I went from 200 x 99 to 140 x $299, So revenue more than 
doubled while my costs dropped by 30%. This had a much larger effect on profit. 

Courage > Intellect. For small businesses, pricing is more about balls than brains. You just 
have to be willing to say a higher number when you get to the end of the sale. 

Hear More Nos, Make More Money. You have to be willing to delay changing your price 
back when you get your first no. If you go from 50% close rate to 30% close rate but you double 
your prices, you make more moncy. But, you'll hear no forty percent more than you did before. So 
you gotta be willing to stomach it. 

Find The Price With Max LTV, Not Max First Purchase. I uscd to say the highest price
