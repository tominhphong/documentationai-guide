# Content Style & Brand Voice

> Source: `.claude/rules/content-style.md`

This guide defines how your agents generate content. It covers hook formulas, trust-building structure, voice guidelines, and call-to-action strategy.

All agents reference these rules when generating video outlines, blog posts, emails, and social content. Edit this section to customize how Claude writes for you.

## Core Premise

**YouTube is your primary lead source.** All content work — blogs, emails, social clips, guides — serves to drive viewers to your channel and convert them into clients. Every piece has a purpose in the content-to-client pipeline.

Your brand stands on **local expertise + honest analysis + specific data**, not on being first or loudest.

## Hook Formula

Your video and blog openings use this structure to grab attention and set expectations:

### The Formula

**Direct Hook** → **Promise** → **Roadmap** → **Credibility Line**

### Example (Pros & Cons Video)

**Hook:** "Tampa saw 23,000 new residents last year. But most people don't realize how limited your neighborhood options actually are."

**Promise:** "You need to know what you're getting into."

**Roadmap:** "In this video, we'll cover the three neighborhoods with the most inventory, what buyers love about each, what catches people off-guard, and who each neighborhood is actually right for."

**Credibility:** "We've helped over 50 buyers relocate to Tampa Bay in the past two years."

### Hook Components

| Component | Purpose | Guidelines |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Direct Hook** | Grab attention in first 3 seconds | Unique fact, surprising statistic, or common pain point. Viewer-focused, not agent-focused. |
| **Promise** | Tell them why to keep watching | "You need to know...," "Most people get this wrong...," "Here's what I wish I'd known..." |
| **Roadmap** | Outline what's coming | "We'll cover three things: [A], [B], [C]" — sets viewer expectations |
| **Credibility Line** | Establish trust immediately | Your actual credential: "Over 8 years helping buyers relocate to Tampa Bay" (specific and provable) |

**Don't:**
- Start with personal stories (save those for proof points later in the video)
- Make it agent-centric ("I've been in real estate for X years...")
- Use vague superlatives ("I'm the best...") — instead, cite specific results

## CON-to-PRO Structure

Trust is built by acknowledging negatives first, then flipping them. This pattern appears throughout your content.

### The Structure

1. **Acknowledge the con** (validate the audience's concern)
2. **Provide context** (why this is actually a factor)
3. **Flip to the pro** (here's what it means for you)

### Examples

**Insurance Costs:**
- Con: "Yes, insurance rates jumped 40% last year in Tampa."
- Context: "That's a real increase, not an exaggeration."
- Pro: "But here's what that means for buyers right now: It's actually a negotiation advantage. Sellers expect this, so you can factorize it into your offer."

**Market Inventory:**
- Con: "The market is tight. There are fewer homes for sale than six months ago."
- Context: "This is true across Tampa Bay."
- Pro: "But for prepared buyers, this is an opportunity: less competition, more negotiating power on price and terms."

**School Performance:**
- Con: "School A didn't rank in the top 20 this year."
- Context: "Rankings fluctuate, and test scores don't measure everything."
- Pro: "But test scores are one factor. Here's what else matters: [specialized programs, teacher reviews, community engagement]. If you're evaluating schools, here's what to look at beyond rankings."

**Why this works:** Audiences instantly detect sugarcoating. When you acknowledge the negative first, you build credibility. They believe the flip more because you didn't hide the con.

## Content Positioning

### What Drives Your Brand

- **Local expertise** — You know Tampa Bay (or your market) deeply: neighborhoods, schools, pricing trends, buyer profiles
- **Personal experience** — Your actual client stories, your real observations, your honest takes
- **Specific data** — Numbers, dates, sources. Never vague: "many" → "47," "recently" → "April 2026"
- **Honest analysis** — You say what others don't because you believe it's true, not because you're chasing views

### What Doesn't Drive Your Brand

- "Nobody else is covering this topic" — Weak positioning. Your brand stands on merit, not novelty.
- "Zero YouTube videos on this" — Still weak. If it matters, someone's probably covered it. Your angle is what's unique.
- Hype or emotional manipulation — Real estate buyers need info, not adrenaline.

### Content Types & Priorities

1. **Search-driven videos** — Neighborhood tours, city pros/cons, cost-of-living comparisons. These rank in YouTube search and Google. They're your engine.
2. **Evergreen guides** — Buyer process, relocation checklists, market updates. Long-tail value.
3. **Repurposed content** — Turn one video into blog + emails + social clips. Multiplier effect on effort.
4. **Lead magnets** — Relocation guides, neighborhood PDFs. Convert watchers into leads.

## Voice Guidelines

Your voice should be:

| Quality | Looks Like | Doesn't Look Like |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Direct** | Every sentence earns its space. No fluff. | Verbose, rambling, filler phrases. |
| **Conversational** | "Here's what I see..." not "It is observed that..." | Corporate jargon, overly formal |
| **Data-driven** | "Homes appreciated 8% last year" | "Homes are appreciating" (vague) |
| **Honest** | "This neighborhood has downsides: [X, Y, Z]" | "Everything about this area is amazing" |
| **Team perspective** | If you're a solo agent, say so. If it's a couple, say so. | Generic realtor voice |
| **Fair Housing compliant** | "Large homes" or "4-bedroom homes" | "Family homes" (demographic steering) |

## CTA (Call-to-Action) Strategy

Use two CTAs per video: one soft mid-video, one direct at the end.

### Mid-Video CTA (Soft)

Offer value, not pressure. This CTA appears naturally in the context.

**Template:**
> "I put together a free [guide / checklist / resource] on this topic. If you want it, link is in the description."

**Examples:**
- "I put together a free relocation checklist if you're seriously considering this move."
- "I've built a neighborhood comparison guide that breaks down schools, pricing, and vibe for each area. Link in the description."

**Why soft:** Mid-video, you want them to keep watching. The goal is engagement, not a click yet.

### End-Video CTA (Direct)

Direct invitation to connect, with booking link.

**Template:**
> "If you're seriously considering `{{AGENT_CITY}}`, let's talk. [LINK: `{{AGENT_BOOKING_LINK}}`]"

**Examples:**
- "If you're seriously considering relocating to Tampa Bay, let's talk. I'll answer your questions and help you make a plan. Link in the bio."
- "If you're looking to buy or sell in the Tampa market, let's schedule a conversation. My calendar link is in the bio."

**Never:**
- "Buy now" or "Contact me immediately" — too pushy
- Assume buyer profile — "If you're a first-time buyer..." excludes investors, move-up buyers, etc.
- Generic language — "Contact a realtor" doesn't build your brand

## Customization

To customize your brand voice:

1. Edit `.claude/rules/content-style.md` in your framework (this file)
2. Change hook formulas, add banned phrases, adjust voice guidelines
3. Restart Claude Code or your next session picks up the changes

Examples:
- Change "CON-to-PRO" structure if it doesn't match your style
- Add phrases you never want to see (e.g., "stunning" if you prefer "spacious")
- Adjust credibility line template if yours is different
