# Real Estate & Fair Housing Compliance

> Source: Framework design principles + external compliance reference

Every piece of content generated by this framework must comply with fair housing laws. This page outlines the built-in safeguards and guidelines.

## Fair Housing Act Basics

The Fair Housing Act (FHA) and Texas Real Estate Commission (TREC) rules prohibit discrimination and "steering" — guiding clients toward or away from neighborhoods based on protected characteristics.

**Protected classes include:**
- Race, color, or national origin
- Religion
- Sex
- Familial status (presence of children)
- Disability
- Sexual orientation
- Gender identity

**What steering looks like:**
- "This neighborhood is perfect for families" (assumes familial status preference)
- "This is a diverse area" (implies racial composition matters to selection)
- "Crime is lower here, good for young families" (combines multiple protected classes)

**What compliance looks like:**
- Factual, objective descriptions: "4 bedrooms, 2,500 sq ft, near downtown"
- Specific data: "School district ranks #3 in the state" (not "great for kids")
- Neutral language: "buyers" not "families," "large home" not "family home"
- Acknowledge real factors without demographic assumptions

## Framework Built-In Compliance

The framework enforces compliance at the agent level:

### Banned Phrases

These phrases **never appear** in generated content:

| Phrase | Why | Use Instead |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| "dream home" | Generic, emotional | Use the actual address or property type |
| "perfect for families" | Steering by familial status | "4 bedrooms, 2,500 sq ft" |
| "family home" | Steering by familial status | "large home" or describe by beds/baths |
| "turnkey" | Implies ease that may not be real | "recently renovated" or describe condition |
| "motivated seller" | Implies financial desperation (fair housing risk) | Describe the property, not the seller |
| "up-and-coming" | Implies gentrification/demographic change | Describe actual amenities or development |

### Mandatory Phrases

These appear in all video outlines and blog posts:

**For neighborhood descriptions:**
- Use "buyers" not "families," "couples," "young professionals," etc.
- Lead with objective facts: location, size, condition, price, amenities
- If mentioning schools, cite rankings or specific programs (not "great for families")

**For CTA and closing:**
- Use: "If you're seriously considering {{AGENT_CITY}}, let's talk"
- Not: "If you're a [family/young professional/investor] looking for..."

### Agent-Level Validation

Before returning generated content, agents check:

```
✓ No banned phrases
✓ Language is neutral (no demographic steering)
✓ Descriptions are factual, not aspirational
✓ Credibility statement is verifiable
✓ CTAs don't assume buyer profile
```

If any flag triggers, the agent flags the issue and asks you to review before publication.

## TREC & Texas-Specific Rules

If you're licensed in Texas:

- **Avoid implied value judgments** — "good schools" is different from "School District A ranks #3 statewide"
- **Disclose material facts** — Don't omit known issues to make a neighborhood sound better
- **Don't use demographic language** — TREC guidance explicitly warns against mentioning racial, ethnic, or family composition in steering context
- **Document your sources** — When citing statistics (crime rates, school rankings), cite the source

## Real Estate Domain Vocabulary

The framework uses domain-specific language correctly:

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Buyer | Anyone considering purchase (neutral) | "Our buyers range from first-time to experienced investors" |
| Market | Geographic area with economic data | "The Tampa Bay market has seen 12% appreciation" |
| Appreciation | Property value increase (factual) | "Homes in this zip code appreciated 8% YoY" |
| Lot size | Square footage of land | "2,500 sq ft lot, 1,800 sq ft home" |
| School district | Geographic school assignment zone | "Located in School District X" |
| Neighborhood amenities | Objective features (parks, shops, transit) | "0.3 miles from downtown, near Publix" |
| Investment property | Property bought for income/appreciation | "Rental yield averages 4% in this zip code" |

## Auditing Your Content

After an agent generates content, audit it for:

1. **Demographic assumptions** — Does it assume buyer profile based on neighborhood type?
2. **Steering language** — Does it guide toward/away from neighborhoods based on protected classes?
3. **Factual accuracy** — Can every claim be sourced or verified?
4. **Credibility** — Is your credential line specific and provable?

If something feels unsafe, don't publish it. Ask Claude to revise with specific guidance.

## Compliance Checklist for Generated Content

Before publishing any blog post, email, or video outline, verify:

- [ ] No banned phrases present
- [ ] Descriptions are factual, not aspirational
- [ ] Language is neutral (no demographic steering)
- [ ] All statistics are cited or sourced
- [ ] Credibility statement is verifiable
- [ ] CTAs don't assume buyer profile
- [ ] No implied value judgments about neighborhoods

## Example: Compliant vs. Non-Compliant

### ❌ Non-Compliant
> "This beautiful neighborhood is perfect for young families looking to raise children. The excellent schools and safe streets make it ideal for families with kids. Perfect family-oriented community."

**Issues:** Repeated family/children steering, vague "excellent" claim, demographic assumption.

### ✅ Compliant
> "This neighborhood offers several notable features: The median home price is $485K, homes are typically 4–5 bedrooms, the local elementary school ranks #2 statewide, and the area has 1.2 parks per 1,000 residents. Home appreciation has averaged 7% annually. If you're seriously considering this market, let's talk about what matters to you in a home."

**Why it works:** Objective facts, no demographic assumptions, specific data, neutral language, inclusive CTA.
