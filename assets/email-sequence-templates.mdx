# Email Sequence Templates

> Source: `templates/email_sequences/welcome_sequence.md` + `templates/email_sequences/nurture_sequence.md`

Email sequence templates define the structure, cadence, and messaging framework for two core lead engagement flows: a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers and an ongoing weekly nurture sequence for warm leads.

The email-writer agent customizes these sequences per agent profile, substituting personal details and local insights while preserving the proven narrative arc.

Cross-reference: `/agent-instructions/email-writer` for the agent workflow that populates these templates.

## Welcome Sequence

**Duration:** 10 days | **Email Count:** 5 | **Goal:** Build trust and capture first conversation

The welcome sequence takes new subscribers (YouTube viewers, lead magnet downloaders, calendar bookers) through a curated storytelling arc: origin story → common mistake → local insight → pattern recognition → value delivery.

### Email 1 — Day 1: The Origin Story

**Purpose:** Hook the reader with authenticity. Establish why the agent exists in this space.

**Subject line pattern:**
- `"here's why I do this differently"`
- `"quick story about how I got into real estate"`
- `"the thing that changed everything for me"`

**Structure:**

1. **Opening** – Jump directly into the story (no preamble).
2. **Story block** – 100-word narrative: what happened, the struggle, the realization that triggered the agent's approach.
3. **Pivot** – `"That's why I built [your approach/brand] — because [lesson from story]"`
4. **CTA** – Invite reply: `"Reply and tell me where you're moving from. I read every single one."`
5. **P.S.** – Quick local fact (e.g., "Did you know Wesley Chapel has 3x the new construction compared to 5 years ago?")

**Length:** Under 200 words.

---

### Email 2 — Day 3: The #1 Mistake

**Purpose:** Position the agent as a problem-solver. Demonstrate expertise through client pattern recognition.

**Subject line pattern:**
- `"the biggest mistake I see [city] buyers make"`
- `"don't do this when moving to [state]"`
- `"I wish someone had told me this"`

**Structure:**

1. **Opening** – Hook with pattern: `"Almost every buyer I work with makes the same mistake in the first week..."`
2. **Story** – Real, anonymized client example (the wrong move and its consequence).
3. **The lesson** – What they should do instead (actionable, specific).
4. **CTA** – Gate a low-friction asset: `"Want my [neighborhood guide / market report]? Just reply 'guide' and I'll send it."`
5. **P.S.** – Seasonal tip or upcoming local event.

**Length:** Under 200 words.

---

### Email 3 — Day 5: The Hidden Gem

**Purpose:** Showcase local knowledge. Paint the picture of a neighborhood newcomers overlook.

**Subject line pattern:**
- `"the neighborhood nobody talks about"`
- `"my favorite spot in [city] — and it's not [obvious area]"`
- `"drove through here yesterday and had to share"`

**Structure:**

1. **Opening** – Immersive scene: start mid-drive or mid-showing (e.g., `"Pulled up to a showing on Oak Ridge this morning..."`).
2. **Story** – Paint the neighborhood picture: street names, vibe, price ranges, who lives there.
3. **What makes it special** – Specific details that trigger interest (e.g., `"Street trees over 15 years old, walkable to [landmark], commute is 12 minutes to [major hub]"`).
4. **CTA** – Offer deeper breakdown: `"I've got a full breakdown of [area] neighborhoods. Want it?"`
5. **P.S.** – Restaurant or activity recommendation in that area.

**Length:** Under 200 words.

---

### Email 4 — Day 7: The Pattern

**Purpose:** Share data-driven insight. Build credibility through aggregated buyer behavior.

**Subject line pattern:**
- `"after helping [X]+ buyers, I noticed something"`
- `"here's what most of my clients end up choosing"`
- `"the [city] buying pattern nobody expects"`

**Structure:**

1. **Opening** – Pattern trigger: `"After helping [number] buyers relocate here, I started noticing a pattern..."`
2. **Insight** – What most buyers think they want vs. what they actually choose (the counter-intuitive finding).
3. **Why** – The real reason (usually practical: commute, schools, lifestyle fit, value retention).
4. **CTA** – Invite engagement: `"Curious which area might be right for you? I've got a 5-question quiz — reply 'quiz'"`
5. **P.S.** – Market stat supporting the pattern (e.g., `"Median appreciation in [neighborhood] over 3 years: 8.2%"`).

**Length:** Under 200 words.

---

### Email 5 — Day 10: The Direct Value

**Purpose:** Close the sequence with concrete value and a conversation invitation.

**Subject line pattern:**
- `"free [resource] for your [city] move"`
- `"I put this together for you"`
- `"everything I know about [area], in one doc"`

**Structure:**

1. **Opening** – Direct and short: `"I put together something I think will help."`
2. **Value** – Describe the resource (relocation guide, market report, neighborhood comparison, etc.). Emphasize what makes it different.
3. **Why it's different** – `"This isn't generic internet stuff — it's from my actual experience helping buyers on the ground here."`
4. **CTA** – Direct link or low-friction reply gate: `"reply for a copy"`
5. **P.S.** – Calendar invitation: `"And if you want to just hop on a quick call, here's my calendar: [booking link]"`

**Length:** Under 200 words.

## Nurture Sequence

**Duration:** Ongoing | **Cadence:** Weekly | **Goal:** Stay top-of-mind, build trust, move warm leads toward conversation when ready

The nurture sequence replaces the welcome sequence once a lead has engaged or completed the 10-day welcome arc. These weekly emails rotate through five themes that reinforce local expertise, transparency, and lifestyle fit.

### Monday: Weekend Observation

**Purpose:** Light, market-aware, builds casual relationship.

**Opening pattern:** `"So I was at [place] Saturday and..."` or `"Three open houses this weekend, and all three had the same problem..."`

**Tone:** Observational, not salesy. Share something noticed in the market (an issue, a trend, a surprise finding).

**Content arc:**
1. Scene-setting observation (what you saw/noticed)
2. Insight or pattern
3. Why it matters for readers

**CTA:** Light — invite thoughts or questions. Not pushy.

---

### Tuesday: Neighborhood Spotlight

**Purpose:** Build comprehensive local knowledge. Give a reason to stay subscribed.

**Structure:**

1. **Open** – Name a specific neighborhood with real details (streets, prices, vibe).
2. **Feature** – Dive into what makes it special (demographics, commute, schools, lifestyle fit).
3. **CON-to-PRO** – Address one real downside and flip it: `"Yes, [con] is real. But here's what most people miss..."`
   - Example: `"Yes, property taxes are higher than the state average. But the neighborhood appreciation more than compensates."`

**CTA:** `"If this sounds like your fit, let me know — I've got [2–3] listings coming soon in this area."`

---

### Wednesday: Honest CON-to-PRO

**Purpose:** Build trust through transparency. Show you understand the downsides and have solutions.

**Topic options:** Insurance costs, traffic, heat/humidity, tourist crowds, flood zones, HOA fees, commute distance.

**Structure:**

1. **Acknowledge the con** – Be direct: `"Yes, [insurance/traffic/heat] is real. I'm not going to pretend it's not."`
2. **Why people miss it** – Reframe thinking: `"But here's what most people miss when they look at the numbers..."`
3. **The flip** – Show the pro side (mitigation, value justification, lifestyle trade-off that's worth it).

**Example:**
- **Con:** High property insurance in coastal areas.
- **Pro:** Newer homes with hurricane-resistant features; flood insurance premiums declining post-2020; investment appreciation offsetting insurance cost increase.

**CTA:** Consultative: `"If this is a sticking point for you, we can model it out. Hit reply."`

---

### Thursday: Client Pattern or Market Data

**Purpose:** Share data-driven insight. Translate numbers into actionable meaning.

**Pattern options:**
- `"3 out of my last 5 buyers ended up in [area] — here's why"`
- `"Median price just hit [X] in [area]. Here's what that actually means for buyers"`
- `"School ratings just updated — here's what changed and why it matters"`

**Structure:**

1. **Data point** – Share specific finding (sales pattern, price movement, metric update).
2. **Translation** – What it means (not just the stat, but the implication for a buyer making a decision now).
3. **Action** – What to do with this insight.

**CTA:** `"If you're serious about [area], we should talk soon before inventory tightens further."`

---

### Friday: Lifestyle Pick

**Purpose:** Show you actually live/work there. Keep tone personal and light.

**Content:** Restaurant, activity, event, or local spot worth knowing about.

**Angle options:**
- `"Aubrey's pick this week: [place] — here's why it matters if you're new here"`
- `"[Event] is coming up on [date] — this is your chance to experience [area] at its best"`
- `"Found this gem last week: [restaurant/activity]. Perfect for families / date night / [lifestyle fit]"`

**CTA:** Light or social: `"Have you tried this spot? Let me know what you think."`

## Seasonal Special Emails

Replace one regular weekly email with timely, high-value seasonal content:

| Season | Timing | Topic | Example CTA |
|--------|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Hurricane** | June | Prep tips, insurance reminders, flood zone explainer | `"Got flood zone questions? Reply and I'll break down what it means for your property."` |
| **Snowbird** | Oct–Nov | Traffic patterns, seasonal pricing shifts, temporary rental options | `"If you're considering a seasonal move, here's what's happening with pricing right now."` |
| **Summer** | Jul–Aug | Heat survival guide, indoor activities, school enrollment deadlines | `"Enrolling kids in a new school? Here's the timeline and what to expect."` |
| **Tax Season** | Jan–Mar | Homestead exemption reminders, property tax tips, deduction strategies | `"New to the area? You might qualify for homestead exemption. Here's how."` |
| **Holiday** | Dec | Year-end market recap, best holiday events, community traditions | `"Here's what I'm grateful for about our market this year — and where I see it heading."` |

## Rules & Best Practices

- **Never send more than 3 emails per week** (weekly nurture + occasional bonus seasonal = max 3)
- **Always include phone number in signature** for easy outreach
- **Never pressure — educate and invite** – Respect lead timeline
- **Rotate between story-driven and data-driven content** – Varies experience and engagement type
- **If a lead replies, switch to personal 1:1 conversation immediately** – Don't send templated follow-up to engaged leads
- **Subject lines should look like a text, not a newsletter** – No ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation, no emojis
- **Use plain text formatting** (no HTML graphics) – Builds authenticity, higher deliverability
- **Send from personal email address, not a marketing platform** – Increases trust and reply rate
