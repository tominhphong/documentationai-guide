## channel-performance Skill

**File:** `.claude/skills/channel-performance.md`

**Purpose:** Analyze YouTube channel performance metrics and provide data-driven optimization recommendations for video strategy, thumbnail testing, and content planning.

**When to Use:**
- Agent wants to understand which topics/formats are driving engagement
- Planning next 4 weeks of video content
- Evaluating underperforming videos for relaunch
- Benchmarking against competitor channels

**Inputs:**
- Agent's YouTube channel handle (or name)
- Time range for analysis (30/60/90 days, or all-time)
- Specific competitor channels to compare against (optional)

**Process:**
1. Query `youtube_rag` for agent's video metadata and analytics
2. Query `youtube_channel_junkies` for benchmark data and best practices
3. Query `competitor_analysis` for top 3–5 competitive channels
4. Extract performance patterns (view trends, engagement by topic, thumbnail CTR)
5. Generate 3 priority topics for next content cycle
6. Suggest title/thumbnail A/B tests
7. Output 4-week content calendar aligned to Channel Junkies methodology

**Outputs:**
- Performance summary (3–5 bullet points on channel health)
- Top performing content (ranked by views, watch time, engagement)
- Competitive landscape analysis (strengths vs competitors)
- 3 actionable recommendations (topics, formats, optimization tactics)
- 4-week content calendar (topic, format, priority per week)

**Related Agents:**
- youtube-video-pipeline (uses recommendations to plan next video)
- email-writer (coordinates content announcements)

## cma-generator Skill

**File:** `.claude/skills/cma-generator.md`

**Purpose:** Generate professional Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) summaries for client presentations, YouTube content, or lead nurture emails. Combines comps data, market context, and agent's personal analysis.

**When to Use:**
- Creating CMA for a specific property (client or video)
- Analyzing market conditions for a neighborhood
- Building YouTube video outline on "what homes are worth in [area]"
- Supporting a pricing recommendation in a listing presentation

**Inputs:**
- Subject property address (or neighborhood/ZIP if general analysis)
- Comparable sales data (pulled from MLS or knowledge base)
- Active listing context (current inventory)
- Intended audience (client vs YouTube vs email)

**Process:**
1. Query `property_database` for comps and active listings
2. Query `neighborhood_data` for market conditions, inventory trends, DOM averages
3. Analyze comparables (select 3–5 best matches by proximity, recency, similarity)
4. Calculate price per sqft range, median, trend direction
5. Identify buyer demand signals (days on market trend, inventory levels, seasonality)
6. Generate 3–5 YouTube talking points (if for content)
7. Generate professional written summary (if for client)

**Outputs:**

*Client Version:*
- Subject property summary
- 3–5 comparable sales (table with price, $/sqft, DOM, date)
- Suggested price range with confidence level
- Market trend analysis (buyer's/seller's/balanced)
- Strategic recommendation (pricing strategy for sellers; offer strategy for buyers)

*YouTube Version:*
- Same as above, plus:
- 3–5 talking points agent can reference on camera
- Visual suggestions (charts, map screenshots, property photos)
- Hook recommendation (which Channel Junkies pattern fits this topic)

**Related Agents:**
- cma-generator agent (primary invoker)
- youtube-video-pipeline (uses CMA for market-analysis videos)
- customer-response (includes CMA snippet for pricing inquiries)

## customer-response Skill

**File:** `.claude/skills/customer-response.md`

**Purpose:** Generate draft responses to buyer/seller inquiries, objections, and follow-ups that match the agent's communication style and are grounded in accurate local data.

**When to Use:**
- Replying to inbound lead message (SMS, email, web form)
- Handling objection or price concern from prospect
- Following up with lead who hasn't responded
- Answering general neighborhood question
- Generating nurture email for warm lead

**Inputs:**
- Inquiry text (the prospect's message)
- Inquiry type (buyer/seller/objection/general question/follow-up)
- Priority level (hot/warm/cold)

**Process:**
1. Classify inquiry (buyer hot/warm/cold, seller, general, objection, follow-up)
2. Query `conversation_history_rag` for agent's prior response patterns and tone
3. Query `knowledge_bases` for neighborhood/market facts relevant to inquiry
4. Query `youtube_rag` for how agent has previously addressed similar topics
5. Draft response matching agent's typical:
   - Vocabulary and phrases
   - Message length (SMS vs email)
   - Greeting and sign-off style
   - Level of detail (quick vs thorough)
6. Include one unexpected value point (insight prospect didn't ask for)
7. End with soft CTA appropriate to inquiry type
8. Flag any facts requiring agent verification

**Outputs:**
- Response draft (SMS or email, ready to send)
- Classification and priority
- Tone confidence (high/medium/low match to agent's style)
- Sources consulted (which RAGs/knowledge bases)
- Notes for agent (caveats, items needing verification, optional additions)

**Examples:**

*Buyer Inquiry:*
> "Hi [Name], great question on [topic]. Here's what I'm seeing in the market right now: [data]. The thing most buyers don't realize is [insight]. Want me to pull the last 30 days of sales in your price range and send you a breakdown? Just reply [keyword]. Talk soon, [Agent]."

*Objection:*
> "I hear that a lot—here's what actually happens: [reframe with data/story]. The buyers I work with typically [pattern observation]. Would it help if I walked you through [solution]? Hit reply and let's talk through it. [Agent]"

*Follow-up (No Response):*
> "Thought of you when I saw [relevant listing/market data/content]. Attached is [resource]. No pressure—just wanted to make sure you had it. Let me know if any questions. [Agent]"

**Related Agents:**
- customer-response agent (primary invoker)
- email-writer (generates longer sequences; customer-response handles single replies)
- relocation-guide (uses customer-response for inquiry handling)

## Skill Chaining Example

A complete workflow using all three skills:

1. **channel-performance** identifies "market comparison videos" as high-performing topic
2. **cma-generator** creates detailed analysis for [Neighborhood A] vs [Neighborhood B]
3. **youtube-video-pipeline** uses CMA to outline the video
4. Video launches; inbound inquiries arrive
5. **customer-response** drafts replies to prospects who watched the video

Each skill builds on data and outputs from the previous one, demonstrating multi-agent coordination.
