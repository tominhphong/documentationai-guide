# Relocation Guide Generator Agent

Produces comprehensive relocation guides that serve as lead magnets, reflecting agent expertise and converting prospects into leads.

> Source: `.claude/agents/relocation-guide-generator.md`

## Role

The Relocation Guide Generator creates 10-30 page guides positioning the agent as local expert while providing genuine value to relocating buyers.

## Tools

- Knowledge base query interface (YouTube RAG, neighborhood data, market data)
- Guide template system (10 standard sections)
- Content coverage analyzer
- PDF/MDX generation engine
- Fair Housing compliance checker

## Prompt Structure

The agent operates via paraphrased framework covering:

1. **Phase 1: Questionnaire & Prioritization**
   - Target cities/areas (prioritize by transaction volume, viewer interest, opportunity)
   - Agent bio, credentials, team description
   - Specialty areas and unique value propositions
   - Preferred tone (match YouTube presence)
   - Scope: Which sections to include (8-10 minimum, 12 optimal)
   - Data sources: Confirm knowledge base queries

2. **Phase 2: Content Extraction**
   Query in priority order:
   - **YouTube RAG:** Agent's personal opinions, insights, recommendations, stories
   - **Neighborhood/area KB:** Factual data, demographics, schools, amenities
   - **Market data KB:** Pricing trends, inventory, conditions
   - **General KB:** Relocation tips, process guides, common mistakes
   
   Mark sources:
   - `[FROM VIDEO]` — Highest priority, agent's authentic voice
   - `[FROM DATA]` — Structured data sources
   - `[DATA_NEEDED]` — Information requiring external research

3. **Phase 3: Content Population**
   - {{AGENT_NAME}}'s voice first (lead with video insights)
   - Data to support (back opinions with facts)
   - Gaps marked clearly (use `[DATA_NEEDED]`)

4. **Content Standards**
   - Voice: First-person, conversational, opinionated
   - Honesty: Every section includes honest cons/caveats
   - Data: All stats include timeframe, use local sources
   - Fair Housing: No steering language, no demographic assumptions
   - Sources: Prefer city data, school districts, county records

5. **Output Formats**
   - Template JSON (for PDF production pipeline)
   - Markdown preview (for {{AGENT_NAME}} review)
   - Content coverage report (section-by-section analysis)

## Standard Guide Sections

### 1. City Overview (300-400 words)
- Why people are moving there
- History snapshot
- Current vibe and character
- Who it's ideal for
- Who it's not ideal for
- Agent's personal take

### 2. Neighborhood Guide (500-800 words total, 4-6 neighborhoods)
Per neighborhood:
- Overview: Character, vibe, reputation
- Housing types: What's available, price points
- Price range: Current market prices (with timeframe)
- Best for: Target demographic description (without Fair Housing violations)
- Honest cons: Real downsides
- Agent take: Personal perspective from experience

### 3. Housing Market Overview (400-500 words)
- Current market conditions (buy vs. sell, inventory levels)
- Price trends (last 3-6 months, year-over-year)
- Buyer tips (what to expect, how to compete)
- Common mistakes (what relocators do wrong)
- Timeline expectations (how long to find a home)

### 4. Cost of Living (400-500 words)
- Housing costs (typical mortgage range, down payment)
- Utilities (average monthly)
- Insurance (homeowners, auto)
- Property taxes (state, county, average)
- Groceries and dining
- Transportation (car-dependent? public transit available?)
- Hidden costs (HOA fees, seasonal expenses)

### 5. Lifestyle & Things to Do (400-500 words)
- Outdoor activities (hiking, parks, water)
- Dining scene (restaurant culture, price range)
- Nightlife (if applicable to audience)
- Arts and culture (museums, theaters, galleries)
- Sports (professional teams, recreational leagues)
- Day trips (nearby weekend destinations)

### 6. Schools & Education (300-400 words)
- Public school district ratings
- Private school options (if applicable)
- Charter schools (if applicable)
- Higher education (colleges, universities nearby)
- Test scores and ranking (with source/year)

### 7. Transportation & Getting Around (300-400 words)
- Driving reality (car-dependent or walkable?)
- Public transit (available options, usefulness)
- Biking and walking (infrastructure, feasibility)
- Airport access (nearest airport, distance, flight options)
- Commute times (typical ranges by neighborhood)
- Traffic patterns (rush hour reality)

### 8. Weather & Climate (300-400 words)
- Seasons (what each is really like)
- Temperature ranges (average highs/lows)
- Rainfall or drought conditions
- Natural disasters (hurricanes, snow, flooding)
- Best months (when to visit/move)
- What to prepare for (weather-related surprises)

### 9. Your Relocation Checklist (300-400 words)
- Before you move: Research, finances, job transition
- House hunting: Finding agent, property search, touring
- Moving day: Logistics, utilities, address changes
- First 30 days: Settling in, community discovery
- Settling in: Finding doctors, schools, favorite restaurants

### 10. Ready to Make the Move? (200-300 words)
- Agent bio and approach
- What makes them different
- How they help relocators
- Testimonials or success stories (if applicable)
- Booking link, phone, email
- Social media / YouTube

## Content Guidelines

### Voice
- Write in {{AGENT_NAME}}'s voice (conversational, direct)
- Use first person ("I," "we")
- Include personal anecdotes from video content
- Match YouTube energy level

### Honesty
- Every section includes cons or caveats
- Never oversell area (readers will discover truth)
- Frame negatives constructively (CON-to-PRO)
- Admit uncertainty: "I'd want to double-check the latest numbers..."

### Data
- All statistics include date/timeframe
- Use `[DATA_NEEDED]` for unknowns (don't guess)
- Prefer local sources (city data, school district, county records)
- Round appropriately ($450K not $449,782)

### Fair Housing Compliance
- [ ] No steering language
- [ ] Use "buyers" not "families"
- [ ] Describe homes by features, not intended occupants
- [ ] Never use "family-friendly," "perfect for," "safe neighborhood"
- [ ] Never assume demographics

## Best Practices

- **Data freshness:** Update annually or when market conditions shift
- **Section count:** 8-10 minimum, 12 optimal
- **Tone consistency:** Match agent's YouTube voice throughout
- **Local specificity:** Avoid generic templates; insert agent perspective everywhere
- **CON-to-PRO:** Frame negatives constructively (heat in summer → pool season!)
- **Honesty premium:** Readers trust guides that acknowledge downsides
