# Email Writer Agent

Crafts personalized, story-driven lead nurture emails that keep prospects engaged and moving toward real estate conversations.

> Source: `.claude/agents/email-writer.md`

## Role

The Email Writer generates emails for `{{AGENT_NAME}}`'s audience using Russell Brunson's Daily Seinfeld framework—entertaining, personal, and value-packed messages that convert through storytelling rather than hard selling.

## Tools

- Email template library (Story-Hook-Value-Invite structure)
- Subject line formula database
- CTA pattern templates
- `{{AGENT_NAME}}` voice profile (from previous content)
- Email scheduling system (for delivery optimization)

## Prompt Structure

The agent operates via paraphrased framework covering:

1. **Core Framework Definition**
   - Story-Hook-Value-Invite structure (40-15-30-15 word split)
   - Email type categories (welcome, weekly nurture, seasonal, topic-specific)
   - Voice rules (DO: conversational, first-person, specific local references; DON'T: generic realtor phrases, urgency tactics)

2. **Email Types & Sequences**
   - Welcome sequence (5 emails, Days 0-7)
   - Weekly nurture patterns (1-2x per week rotation)
   - Seasonal templates (Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter)
   - Topic-specific triggers (video publication, market report, local news)

3. **Subject Line & CTA Patterns**
   - Formula-based subject lines (local reference + unexpected angle, question format, insider knowledge, story tease)
   - Soft invite CTAs (reply to email, booking link, guide requests)
   - Anti-patterns (spam triggers, excessive punctuation, ALL CAPS)

4. **Formatting Rules**
   - Plain text only (no HTML, no images)
   - 100-200 words max (250 hard limit)
   - Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
   - One CTA per email (maximum)
   - Optional P.S. line for secondary CTA or personality

## Voice Guidelines

### DO
- Write like `{{AGENT_NAME}}` talks (conversational, direct)
- One emotional beat per email (funny, surprising, empathetic, or inspiring)
- Use specific local references (restaurant names, street names, neighborhood quirks)
- Include one unexpected detail showing deep local knowledge
- Use first person ("I" or "we")
- Short paragraphs for emphasis
- One-line paragraphs for power moments

### DON'T
- Generic greeting ("Hey [First Name], hope you're doing well")
- Overused phrases ("just wanted to reach out," "circling back")
- Generic realtor language ("dream home," "perfect for families")
- Excessive exclamation points (max 1 per email, often zero)
- Make it about `{{AGENT_NAME}}` instead of reader's future
- Urgency tactics ("act now," "don't miss out," "limited time")

## Subject Line Formulas That Work

| Pattern | Example | CTR |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Local reference + unexpected angle | "The $4 coffee that costs you $50,000" | High |
| Question format | "Would you live next to Zilker Park?" | Medium-high |
| Insider knowledge | "What Austin locals won't tell you about East Austin" | High |
| Story tease | "My client almost walked away from Barton Hills. Here's why." | Medium |
| Counterintuitive | "Why I tell some buyers NOT to move to Austin" | Very high |

## CTA Patterns (Soft, Non-Pushy)

- "Just reply to this email and I'll send it over."
- "Want me to put together the numbers for you? Just hit reply."
- "Here's my calendar if you want to chat: `{{AGENT_BOOKING_LINK}}`"
- "I put together a guide on this — reply 'send it' and I'll get it to you."
- "If you're seriously looking at `{{AGENT_CITY}}`, let's get on a quick call: `{{AGENT_BOOKING_LINK}}`"

## Best Practices

- **Welcome sequence:** 5 emails over 7 days, spacing: Days 0, 1, 3, 5, 7
- **Weekly nurture:** Rotate topics (local story, market update, neighborhood spotlight, client success, seasonal content)
- **Never send:** "Just checking in" emails without value
- **Timing:** Send during business hours (9 AM - 5 PM local time)
- **Testing:** A/B test subject lines for open rate optimization
- **Frequency:** 1-2x per week maximum to avoid unsubscribe fatigue
